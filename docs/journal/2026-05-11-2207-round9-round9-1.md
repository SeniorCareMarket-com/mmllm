**Monotonic-decay pattern: broken.** The trajectory is non-monotonic. After
the cycle-1 peak (step 500, Δ_net=0.94 amid a ctrl spike), Δ_net decays to
~0.17 around step 4000, then **recovers** to 0.28 by step 5000. Final Δ_net

worker Δ_net at the same step count.

Three inflection points:
- **Step 500**: ctrl_bpc divergence-and-recovery; Δ_net spikes to 0.94
  against a wrecked baseline. Treat as transient.
- **Step 2750**: ctrl spikes to 0.503, then Δ_local jumps to 0.137 (first
  substantial Local contribution all run) and Δ_net climbs back to 0.72.
  Likely the LR_NET cosine crossing some threshold around 2×.
- **Step 4250–5000**: Δ_net recovers from 0.17 to 0.28 while ctrl drops
  to 0.28. Net contributes growing share as the model stabilizes at the
  end of the LR_NET cosine ramp.

**Synergy** (Δ_both − Δ_local − Δ_net): grows from ~0 at step 250 to
**+0.313 at step 5000**. The two tiers aren't just both contributing —
they contribute *more together* than the sum of their parts. This is the
clearest sign yet that Local and Net occupy genuinely different niches.

absolutes instead. Sign convention is misleading here, not a real

### Gap analysis

#### What the residual target did NOT fix

**Cross-cycle decay is still present**, just from a much higher peak.
Δ_net went 0.94 → 0.17 over steps 500→4000 — a 5.5× drop. Then
recovered to 0.28 by step 5000. The "monotonic decay after cycle-1 peak"
pattern is **partly broken** (there's a late-run recovery) but the
underlying decay is still there during the middle of the run.

**ctrl_bpc stability is worse**, not better. Three spikes during this
run (1.06, 0.50, 0.54) vs none in round-8. The residual target makes Net
mechanically more relevant, but the training trajectory is noisier.

#### Per-layer asymmetries that look like real mechanism

**L0/L1 inversion** is the clearest signal:
- L0 routes Net (w_net=0.55) but barely fires Local (firing=0.08)
- L1 fires Local heavily (firing=0.70) but suppresses Net (w_net=0.011)

These are **complementary specializations**, not random divergence. L0
has decided "Net is the bank tier I want"; L1 has decided "Local is the
bank tier I want." The gate + Bernoulli combination found this
differentiation without any direct constraint.

**L2 = sdpa-dominant** suggests this layer found that neither bank tier
was helping above the sdpa baseline. That's also informative — it means
the architecture *can* down-route the bank where it doesn't help, which
is what we want.

**α_net climbs to ~1 in L1–L3** but stays at 0.27 in L0. L0 alone
decided Net's output didn't need amplification (because w_net is already
0.55 of gate weight — Net is loud-enough on its own). L1–L3 amplify Net
when the gate routes to it.

#### Bug suspicions

1. **`consolidation_idx` baseline scaling**: Δ_local at step 250 was
   +0.0002 (essentially noise), and that becomes the divisor for every
   subsequent ratio. Result: all 20 ratios are -65 to -800. This is a
   computation defect, not a model issue — the baseline epsilon should
   be clamped to (e.g.) max(|Δ_local|, 0.01) before forming the ratio.

2. **The step-500 ctrl spike** to 1.06 looks like genuine numerical
   instability. It happens at a step where the replay buffer just
   filled (the router-metrics line at step ~500 shows `replay_buf=256/256`
   for the first time). Could be that replay-CE going from a warm-up
   regime to full-buffer regime briefly causes a learning-rate-effective
   jump.

3. **Synergy growing to +0.313** is suspicious in its own way: if Net
   and Local truly factor independently, synergy should be 0. The fact
   that Δ_both = Δ_local + Δ_net + 0.313 means there's a nonlinear
   interaction — possibly the gate softmax that re-normalizes between
   tiers when Local fires vs doesn't (different normalization paths
   when one input is gated off).

#### Mechanism hypothesis for Δ_net's decay during steps 1000–4000

**Net is learning a static target**: with residual distill, the target
is `(local_out − sdpa_out).detach()`. As training proceeds, dense+sdpa
gradually absorb whatever Local is adding (because Local's gradient
flows into the model through the SwitchGate and the o_proj). The
residual `(local_out − sdpa_out)` shrinks in magnitude as dense
converges. Net then learns a shrinking target.

This is consistent with: ‖net_out‖ is large (13–32 per layer at step
5000) but its **contribution after the gate** is small in L1 and L2
(w_net ≈ 0.01) because the gate has decided sdpa+Local cover those
layers' work. Net's V holds learned content, but the router routes
around it.

The late-run recovery (steps 4250→5000) coincides with the LR_NET
cosine peaking near 5× — Net pushes back into the gate weight by
having larger updates. That's why Δ_net climbs 0.17 → 0.28 in the
last 1000 steps.

#### One concrete fix to test next

**Freeze (or near-freeze) dense at step 1000, then continue.** Set
`MMLLM_LR_DENSE_MULT=0.05` (or even 0) for steps 1000+, leave LR_BANK
and LR_NET as-is. If the mechanism hypothesis above is right, dense
absorbing Local's signal is what shrinks the distill residual target;
freezing dense after warmup keeps the target meaningful and Δ_net
should plateau or rise rather than decay.

**Telemetry that would confirm**: ‖local_out − sdpa_out‖ should stop
shrinking. If it stays steady (or grows) across steps 1000–5000 with
dense frozen, and Δ_net plateaus accordingly, the residual-shrinkage
hypothesis is confirmed. If ‖local_out − sdpa_out‖ shrinks anyway
(driven by Local converging to sdpa directly without dense help),
the bottleneck is in the bank tier itself and a different fix is
needed (e.g., divergence-penalty between Local and sdpa).

### One honest take on this round

Architecturally, **this is the most differentiated, most synergistic
bank tier of any round** — first time both tiers carry meaningful
contribution and synergy is non-trivial. The residual-target distill
genuinely broke the Net=Local twin basin.

But it didn't move the agentic-eval needle, and ctrl_bpc is noisier
than round-8. Net carrying 0.28 bpc of unique signal is great for the
architectural thesis ("the bank tier is doing work that dense can't
reproduce") but it doesn't translate to format_validity gain. Format
validity is plausibly an emergent property of *dense weight specific
state* in the round-6 community core, and any single-worker training
(no matter how architecturally healthy) moves dense weights away from
that state. The fix likely isn't more bank-tier architecture; it's
either (a) freeze dense entirely after step 0 — train only bank tiers —
or (b) merge across many workers and let the FedAvg average snap dense
back near the round-6 core.
