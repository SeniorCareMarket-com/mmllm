## Narrative

**Headline: Δ_net = -0.0001 at step 5000. Round-3 hypothesis NOT met
(target was Δ_net > 0.01).**

But the result is not what it looks like at face value. The script's
resume mechanism didn't actually fire. Here's the trace:

1. `scripts/preflight_fim_run.sh` correctly auto-discovered
   `core/round-2/step-20000/dense.pt` and wrote `RESUME_FROM=...` to
   `/tmp/mmllm-cpu/preflight.env`.
2. `scripts/run_round2.sh` then created `${FIM_BASE}.ckpts/step-0/` and
   copied the resume dense.pt into it.
3. `train-fim` runs through to `train-long`, which calls
   `(latest-checkpoint-step ckpt-dir)` to decide whether to resume. It
   returns `0` (the only step- dir found). Then
   `(when (pos? start-step) (load-checkpoint! ...))` skips the load,
   because 0 isn't positive. The dense.pt at step-0/ was never loaded.
4. The NetBank warm-start branch still fires — but warm-start projects
   *Local Bank's V* into NetBank's V. Local V is random init at this
   point (since we didn't resume). The "V warm-start from Local V" fix
   that round-3 was specifically meant to test was effectively a no-op.

So the round-3 result is from training-from-scratch on the synth corpus
under the sleep-cycle schedule, not from a community-core continuation.

### Symptoms of the divergence

- step 1000 val_bpc = 16.23 (worse than uniform-byte baseline of 8 bpc)
- loss bouncing 10–15 from steps 0–2500
- sudden recovery at step ~2550 when the cosine cools `lr_b` from
  3.0e-2 → ~1.6e-2, below the divergence threshold for the freshly-init
  Local Bank
- by step 3000 loss is ~0.4, ctrl bpc ~0.66; by step 5000 ctrl bpc 0.40
- but only 2500 effective training steps means total signal absorbed is
  much less than rounds 1/2

### Recommendation for round 4

The single-line fix needed in `scripts/run_round2.sh`:

```diff
- mkdir -p "${FIM_BASE}.ckpts/step-0"
- cp "${RESUME_FROM}" "${FIM_BASE}.ckpts/step-0/dense.pt"
+ mkdir -p "${FIM_BASE}.ckpts/step-1"
+ cp "${RESUME_FROM}" "${FIM_BASE}.ckpts/step-1/dense.pt"
```

Place the seed dense.pt at `step-1/` (or any positive N) so
`latest-checkpoint-step` returns `pos?` and resume actually fires.
Alternative: patch `core.lpy` to load step-0 too — but that's a
pipeline change, not a script fix.

Once resume fires, the V warm-start should actually have round-2's
trained V to project from. Until then, the round-3 hypothesis remains
untested in this run.

### What WAS demonstrated

- The diversified synth corpus works (script generates 20k JSON
  records from prep_xlam_synth.py).
- NetBank V warm-start code path fires when enabled (logs show "K_a/K_b
  + V (projected)" — round-2's log only said "K_a/K_b").
- The flat global cosine (MMLLM_LR_MIN=3e-3 = max) successfully kept
  NetBank's absolute effective LR at 3e-2 at the end of the sleep
  cycle. lr_n peaked at 3.00e-2 at step 5000 (round-2's hit ~3e-3 at
  end due to global decay).
- The 10× LR multiplier on Local Bank is destabilizing when the
  underlying model is at random init. Round-2 ran at 10× LR on top of a
  partially-trained model and worked; round-3 ran at 10× LR on random
  init and diverged for the first half of training.

### Pipeline notes

- agent-eval took 870s wall (~14.5 min) for 100 samples × 256 gen byte
  — NetBank's simulated 1-10ms WAN delay per forward + cpu-tiny's slow
  inference combine to ~3.4s/sample.
- fim-eval also took ~15 min (greedy decode max_middle=256 × 11
  examples through the slower NetBank forward).
- The script tried to commit `workers/round3-a/step-5000/fim-bank-net.*.bin`
  (128 MB each × 4 layers = 512 MB) which exceeded GitHub's 100 MB
  blob limit; rejected on push. Removed those before re-commit. The
  WORKERS.md guidance ("don't commit per-worker bank state") suggests
  the `cp ${BANK_BASE}-net.*.bin "$WORKER_DIR/"` line in run_round2.sh
  should be reconsidered — bank V state is per-worker and
  reconstructable from training, not for sharing.
