consolidation phase of this run?** Retrospective analysis on the structured log
(`workers/round10-4/step-5000/log.jsonl`, 65 records, no new training). The earlier
"Narrative" section above answered "did LR=50 destabilize" — the right question for the
sweep but not for the architecture. This section answers the architectural question.

### Q1. Did Δ_net rise while Δ_local plateaued or dropped?

**No.** The opposite happened. In the back half:

- **Δ_local rose, did not drop.** +0.0039 → +0.0068, a +75% relative climb. Mean
  +0.0067 across the 11 back-half ablations, with a steady upward drift after step 2500
  rather than a plateau or decline.
- **Δ_net declined slightly, did not rise.** +0.0572 → +0.0532, a −7% relative drift.
  Range [+0.0527, +0.0572] — narrow, basically flat, with a faint downward slope.

This is the **inverse** of the consolidation signature the triune-brain architecture is
designed to produce. A successful consolidation would show Δ_local falling (Local
ceding accumulated content) and Δ_net rising (Net absorbing it). What actually happened
is the symmetric opposite: Local stayed plastic in the cooldown and quietly gained,
while Net's ablation-detectable contribution drifted slightly *down*. The wake-phase
peak in Δ_local at step 2000 (+0.0086) did not transfer; it bled off into Local-local
storage and stayed there.

The `consolidation_idx` field name is misleading here: it measures Δ_local relative to
the step-250 baseline (−0.0076), not actual transfer. A high consolidation_idx in this
run means "Local accumulated more content than baseline," not "content moved from Local
to Net." Across the back half it sat at +1.5 to +2.0 — a perfectly steady Local
accumulation signal, with no corresponding Net rise.

### Q2. If yes, where can we see the transfer in telemetry?

Strictly speaking, no — Δ_net did not rise. But there *is* a structural shift in the
back-half per-layer telemetry that's worth flagging, even though it didn't translate to
ablation-detectable transfer.

Per-layer telemetry averaged across all 4 layers, back-half start → end (step 2500 →
5000):

| signal | step 2500 | step 5000 | Δ |

| ‖local_out‖ | 56.43 | 55.57 | **−1.5%** |
| ‖net_out‖ | 3.36 | 4.44 | **+31.9%** |
| ‖net_V_grad‖ | 0.1604 | 0.0615 | **−61.7%** |
| gate_w_local | 0.2204 | 0.2078 | −5.7% |
| gate_w_net | 0.1888 | 0.1955 | +3.6% |

| α_net | 0.5318 | 0.5287 | −0.6% |

There are three signals here that, on their own, look consolidation-like:

1. **‖net_out‖ grew +32% in the back half** (+52% over the whole run; 2.92 → 4.44).
   Net's output magnitude is monotonically increasing.
2. **‖local_out‖ declined −1.5% in the back half** (after peaking at step 1000 at
   58.23; back-half range [55.5, 56.4]). Local's output magnitude has flatlined or
   slightly retreated.
3. **‖net_V_grad‖ collapsed −62%** (back half) and **−97% over the whole run** (2.14
   → 0.06). Net's V parameter is no longer under gradient pressure — it has settled.
4. **Routing gate shifted away from Local by 5.7% and toward Net by 3.6%.** A modest but
   directionally-correct redistribution.

These four signals together describe **a system where Net is growing in scale and
settling its internal state, while Local is contracting modestly and gate-routing is
nudging away from it**. Structurally this looks consolidation-shaped.

But it didn't translate to ablation evidence. ‖net_out‖ can grow without Net's V
acquiring more *useful information* — the dense weights downstream can simply learn to
scale the existing Net contribution. The fact that ‖net_V_grad‖ also collapsed is
consistent with this: Net's V stopped *changing*, suggesting it reached the boundary of
what its current training objective could teach it. ‖net_out‖ growing while ‖net_V_grad‖
falls is the signature of "downstream scaling, not Net learning."

So the answer to Q2 is: **the magnitude/routing telemetry shows consolidation-shaped
drift, but Δ_net is the only metric that measures actual functional transfer, and Δ_net
says no.**

### Q3. If no — which it likely was — why didn't migration happen?

Four mechanism failures, listed in order of confidence:

**(a) Direction-only distill is the smoking gun.** `MMLLM_DISTILL_DIRECTION_ONLY=true`
makes the distill loss match only the *cosine direction* of Local's residual, not its
magnitude. This means:

- distill tells Net "rotate your output to align with the direction Local produces"
- distill *cannot* tell Net "absorb this specific magnitude of contribution"
- so even if Local has accumulated content with magnitude m in a useful direction d̂,
  direction-only distill teaches Net only d̂

This is exactly the telemetry pattern we see: ‖net_out‖ grew (Net rotated toward
Local's direction, dense weights amplified) but Δ_net under ablation stayed flat (Net's
V doesn't actually hold Local's content). Direction-only distill was added in round-9
v3 to fix the multiplicative-feedback instability of the earlier residual-magnitude
distill (the "v1 sign bug" / multiplicative-on-x feedback path). It bought stability at
the cost of disabling the channel through which content transfers. **This is the
architectural trade-off this run quietly demonstrates.**

**(b) The LR schedule has insufficient overlap window.** The schedule design intends
Local to cool while Net ramps. The numbers from the train log:

| step | lr_b (Local) | lr_n (Net) |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 250 | 1.50e-01 | 4.20e-05 (cold start) |
| 2500 | 7.20e-02 | 1.73e-03 |
| 4000 | 1.44e-02 | 2.71e-03 |
| 4750 | 9.34e-04 | 2.98e-03 |
| 5000 | 3.01e-06 | 3.00e-03 (~peak) |

Local is essentially frozen by step 4500 (lr_b ≈ 4e-3, 97% cooled). Net's LR is at
2.93e-3 by then. The "Local frozen, Net plastic" window is steps 4500-5000 ≈ 500 steps
≈ 10% of the run. Even if Net had a working content-absorption channel, it doesn't have
enough plastic time to use it. The LR_NET ramp peaks at 1.0× the dense base at step
5000, but the dense base is 1.5e-5 at that point — Net's effective LR is microscopic
when Local finally yields.

**(c) Bernoulli gate caps the distill information rate.** Local fires ~27% of the time
(`local_firing_rate ≈ 0.272`, stable across the run). On the other 73% of steps,
Local's residual contribution is gated to zero and distill has no signal. The
information channel from Local to Net through distill is rate-limited to 27% of
training tokens. Combined with direction-only, the bandwidth is "27% of steps × direction-only" — a thin pipe.

**(d) `distill_target=residual` is correct but composes poorly with direction-only.**
The residual target is right — what we want Net to learn is the *correction* Local
applies to the base output, not the base output itself. But direction-only distill on a
residual target loses the magnitude scaling that makes residuals semantically real. A
better composition would be `target=residual + direction_only=false + small distill_coef`
(so magnitude pressure exists but doesn't explode the multiplicative loop). Or
`target=residual + magnitude_aware_distill` if such a flag existed.

### Round-11 recommendation (revised in light of consolidation finding)

The earlier "push LR_BANK to 75-300" recommendation in the Narrative section is
**de-prioritized**. Raising LR_BANK strengthens the wake-phase Local accumulation that
doesn't transfer — it makes the symptom this post-mortem identifies *worse*, not
better.

The architectural single-knob test for round-11 should be:

**Set `MMLLM_DISTILL_DIRECTION_ONLY=false` and re-run round10-4 at LR_BANK_MULT=50,
otherwise identical config.** This isolates the direction-only flag as the suspected
migration blocker. Acceptance signal: Δ_local declines in the back half while Δ_net
rises by at least the amount Δ_local declines. Failure modes to watch:

- early ctrl_bpc excursion > 0.6 → magnitude distill has re-triggered the v1
  multiplicative-feedback bug; back off `distill_coef` from 1.0 to 0.3 and retry
- NaN in distill_loss → same; the direction-only flag was added for a reason and we
  may need the magnitude path soft-clipped (e.g. distill on `tanh(residual)` or with a
  small `distill_coef`)
- Δ_local rises and Δ_net also rises → magnitude distill is moving content but in
  addition to Local accumulation, not in place of it; needs LR-overlap-window fix (see
  below)

If direction-only is confirmed as the blocker, the follow-up sweep should test
**LR_NET ramp shapes that give Net more plastic time *after* Local cools**. Concretely:
delay the LR_BANK cooldown (cosine over the first 60% of the run instead of the full
run) and extend the LR_NET ramp peak through the last 40%. This creates a larger
"Local frozen, Net plastic" window for actual consolidation to occur.

### Honest summary

Round-10 worker 4 produced a stable run with substantial Local accumulation, no NaN,
clean cosine-cooled trajectories, and visually impressive per-layer specialization in
the routing gates and magnitude telemetry. It also produced **zero consolidation
transfer** as measured by the ablation Δ. The architecture executed every motion of
the triune-brain dance except the one it was named for. The most likely cause is the
direction-only distill flag, which was a round-9-v3 stability fix that quietly disabled
the content-transfer channel. The path forward is not more LR sweeps — it is testing
whether removing that flag (carefully, with magnitude-pressure controls) restores the
mechanism the architecture was designed to demonstrate.
