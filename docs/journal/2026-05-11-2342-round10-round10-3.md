### Gap analysis

#### Did this LR produce instability?

**Yes, at step 250.** Δ_local = -0.0216 means Local was actively *hurting* the model at that point. The cosine cooldown brought Local back to noise floor by step 750 and to positive territory by step 1000. ctrl_bpc bumped to 0.345 at step 250 (vs ~0.32 for round9-4 at the same step) — small spike, well under the 0.6 watch threshold, recovered cleanly.

The signature is **early destabilization, not late explosion** (round9-0 comm's failure mode). Direction-only distill + lower LR_NET kept the late-run dynamics calm; the issue is just that 40× LR at step 0 is too hot for Local's V to keep up with stable forward state.

#### Does higher LR_BANK produce more Δ_local? **No, in fact the opposite.**

The naive expectation was "more LR → more accumulation → larger Δ_local." Result: **higher LR_BANK kills accumulation** because:

1. Early-step instability writes noise into V.
2. The Bernoulli gate sees Local's output as noisy and learns to never invoke it (firing_rate=0).
3. Without invocations, Local can't accumulate forward gradient signal (the path is gated off).
4. By the time cosine cools the LR, the gate has settled on never-fire, and Local's V drifts without doing useful work.

Round9-4 ops at LR=10 had bicameral firing (L1=44%, L3=75%) and Δ_local=+0.0386. Round10-3 at LR=40 has zero firing everywhere and Δ_local=+0.0056. **A 4× higher LR_BANK produced 7× less Δ_local.**

#### Does Net follow with the consolidation transfer?

**Yes, but oddly.** Δ_net plateaued at ~+0.048 (vs round9-4's ~+0.085). Lower than round9-4. But per-layer routing diversified — Net got non-trivial gate weight in all 4 layers, whereas round9-4 confined Net to L0 only. So Net is doing less per-layer work, but spread across more layers. Net effect on Δ_net: lower total, but more architecturally distributed.

The consolidation-transfer hypothesis predicted "Local's accumulated content moves to Net via distill before each sleep cycle." With sleep removed and Local barely accumulating anything (gate never fires), there's nothing for distill to transfer. The high LR_BANK broke the consolidation pipeline by starving Local of forward usage.

### Concrete next test for round-11

**Single-knob recommendation: `MMLLM_LR_BANK_MULT_INIT_RAMP_STEPS=500` — a 500-step linear warmup from 1× to the target LR_BANK_MULT before cosine cooldown kicks in.**

The mechanism: the early-step destabilization is the entire problem here. Local's V at step 0 is approximately the round-6 FedAvg state, which is delicately balanced. Hitting it with LR=40 immediately introduces noise faster than the forward state can adapt, and the Bernoulli gate locks Local out within ~250 steps. If we ramp from 1× → 40× over the first 500 steps, Local's V can adapt to its new training distribution gradually, the gate has time to confirm Local is useful, and firing_rate should stay non-zero across the run.

**Telemetry that would confirm:**
- Δ_local at step 250 should be **positive** (warmup keeps LR low when V is fragile).
- local_firing_rate at L1 and L3 should be > 0.3 (matching round9-4 ops's pattern).
- Δ_both should land around +0.10-0.15 instead of +0.055 — Local actually contributing again.

If that works, then a sweep of LR_BANK with the same warmup (10/20/30/40/50 → cosine cool to 0.001, all with 500-step warmup) is the right next experiment. Without warmup, the 4× LR_BANK ceiling looks like it's between LR=10 and LR=40 — the sweep is wasted compute below the warmup fix because the failure mode is bounded by the step-0 hit, not by the LR magnitude itself.

**Alternative single-knob if warmup isn't acceptable: `MMLLM_NET_V_WARMSTART_FROM_LOCAL=true`** — when Local destabilizes early and never recovers, at least give Net's V a copy of Local's pre-instability state at step 0, so Net carries the round-6 inheritance even when Local's gate gets locked out. The current behavior leaves Net's V cold at random init while Local gets a warm start; if Local can't be trusted at high LR, transferring the warm start to Net might preserve more of round-6's capability.

### Honest summary

This is the cleanest "high LR_BANK" data point: LR=40 produces early-step instability that recovers, no late explosion, and a fim_eval that beats every prior single-worker number despite worse bank-tier accumulation than round9-4 ops. **The dispatch's question — "how high can LR_BANK go" — has a partial answer: 40× is past the useful ceiling for Local accumulation, but not past the catastrophic-failure ceiling.** The mechanism that limits Local at high LR isn't an explosion; it's that the Bernoulli gate locks Local out within 250 steps and never re-engages.

The narrow finding: **lower LR_BANK + warmup is probably better than this sweep's setpoints, but full corpus matters more than LR_BANK** for the fim_eval objective. The architecturally-interesting finding: **Net naturally diversifies to all 4 layers when trained on a diverse corpus**, without needing the `GATE_W_NET_FLOOR` knob round9-1/round9-4 proposed. The harvest of all 5 round-10 workers should test whether the LR sweep matters more or less than the corpus expansion, and the FedAvg merge should give the first single-worker-stable trajectory's worth of artifacts on a full-corpus base.

### Q1: Did Δ_net rise while Δ_local plateaued or dropped?

**No. The opposite happened.** Δ_local rose slightly and Δ_net dropped slightly. Both individual contributions are nearly flat, but the direction of motion is anti-consolidation — Local's share grew, Net's share shrank. By any reasonable read of "signal migrating from Local to Net," this run shows the inverse.

Verdict: **migration did not happen.** Not partial. Not slow. Net is doing slightly less work at step 5000 than it was doing at step 2500.

### Q2: If migration didn't happen, where in the telemetry can we see why?

Five independent signatures point at the same conclusion: **Net was learning the main task directly, not absorbing Local's content.**

**Signature 1 — Net's ‖output‖ per layer grew across the back half, but in directions distill wasn't pulling.**

| layer | ‖net_out‖ @ step 2500 | ‖net_out‖ @ step 5000 | growth |
|------:|----------------------:|----------------------:|-------:|
| L0 | 10.52 | 10.44 | -1% (flat) |
| L1 | 0.50 | **1.09** | **+118%** |
| L2 | 0.45 | **0.95** | **+111%** |
| L3 | 1.41 | **2.14** | **+52%** |

Net's outputs in L1/L2/L3 doubled in magnitude. So **Net IS accumulating content** in those layers across the back half — it's not idle, it's actively learning. The question is what it's learning.

**Signature 2 — distill_loss grew 90% across the back half, not decayed.**

If Net were absorbing Local's residual content, the MSE-direction distill loss should monotonically *decrease* as Net's normalized output rotates into alignment with `(local_out − sdpa_out).detach()`. Instead the loss grew from 0.0113 to 0.0214. **Net is moving AWAY from the distill target's direction**, not toward it. The main-task cross-entropy gradient (~0.3 magnitude) overwhelms the direction-only distill gradient (~0.02 × coef 1.0 = 0.02), so Net's V optimizes the next-token objective directly while distill pulls weakly in a direction the main loss doesn't reward.

**Signature 3 — gate weights are frozen across the back half.**

| layer | gate_w_net @ 2500 | gate_w_net @ 5000 | drift |
|------:|------------------:|------------------:|------:|
| L0 | 0.42 | 0.42 | 0.00 |
| L1 | 0.09 | 0.09 | 0.00 |
| L2 | 0.03 | 0.03 | 0.00 |
| L3 | 0.13 | 0.10 | -0.03 |

Net's per-layer routing share is **fixed to 3 decimal places** across 2500 steps of training. Even if Net's V had absorbed something useful, the gate isn't moving to give Net a bigger share of the forward path — so any internal consolidation would be invisible at the output. The consolidation pipeline requires the gate to *redirect* as content moves; with frozen gates, content can move but the model can't exploit it.

**Signature 4 — α_net is fixed.**

| layer | α_net @ 2500 | α_net @ 5000 |
|------:|-------------:|-------------:|
| L0 | 0.28 | 0.28 |
| L1 | 0.62 | 0.62 |
| L2 | 0.59 | 0.59 |
| L3 | 0.64 | 0.62 |

α_net is the per-head scalar that compensates for NetBank's c_net bottleneck. Same frozen-stable picture — not rising as Net's V grows. The amplifier on Net's output path isn't adjusting upward, so even doubled ‖net_out‖ in L1/L2/L3 gets attenuated through the same α_net it had at step 2500.

**Signature 5 — Local was gated off the entire run.**

`local_firing_rate = 0.000` at every layer, every telemetry event, including the back half. **Local was never actually invoked in the forward pass.** Its V was updated by SparseAdam through the ablation-path retrieval (which queries V regardless of gate), but its output never entered the residual stream. The distill target `(local_out − sdpa_out).detach()` is computed with Local nominally on, so distill *does* have a non-zero target — but in production forward, Local is effectively zero. There is no "Local hill-climbed during wake, then Net absorbed it during cool." Local never climbed; the Bernoulli gate locked it out by step 250 and never re-opened.

### Q3: Why didn't migration happen? Concrete mechanism failures.

The consolidation pipeline this architecture is designed around has six requirements. **This run satisfies one.**

| Requirement | Status in this run |
|-------------|--------------------|
| 1. Local must be active during wake (forward path, accumulating content) | ✗ **gate_w_local = 0 at L0/L1/L2 by step 250; firing_rate = 0.000 everywhere.** Local was gated off the entire wake phase. |
| 2. LR_BANK cools, LR_NET ramps (relative plasticity shifts) | ✓ LR_BANK 40 → 0.001 cosine, LR_NET 0.001 → 1.0 cosine, both schedules fired as configured. |
| 3. The distill target must reflect Local's unique contribution | ✗ With Local gated off, local_out ≈ sdpa_out in forward, so `(local_out − sdpa_out).detach()` ≈ 0 vector. Net is being asked to learn the direction of ~zero. The target is structurally vacuous when Local is silent. |
| 4. distill_loss must decay as Net rotates toward the target | ✗ distill_loss **grew 90%** in the back half. Net was moving away, not toward. |
| 5. The gate must redirect routing as content quality shifts | ✗ Gate weights frozen to 3 decimal places across 2500 steps. |
| 6. α_net must scale up as Net's content matures | ✗ α_net frozen across all 4 layers across the back half. |

**Six failures of mechanism, one root cause:** the Bernoulli gate locked Local out within 250 steps under LR_BANK=40. Once Local was off the forward path, requirements 1, 3, and (via the gate's static decision) 5+6 all failed together. The only consolidation knob that did its job mechanically (the LR schedules, requirement 2) was firing into a pipeline whose other components had already collapsed.

**Where did Net's accumulating content come from then?** From the main next-token loss. Net's V grew in magnitude in L1/L2/L3 because cross-entropy backprop loaded those layers with content useful for prediction — but that content is what dense+sdpa was *also* learning, redundantly. Net is becoming a parallel-path dense layer, not absorbing Local's complement. The architecture's hypothesis ("Net learns Local's unique contribution beyond sdpa") didn't get tested in this run because Local never had a unique contribution to begin with.

**The one design feature most responsible:** the Bernoulli gate's hard on/off behavior combined with the high LR_BANK setpoint. The 3-way softmax round-9 used (before the Bernoulli change) had a continuous w_local that couldn't fully zero out — Local was always at least slightly invoked, so it always had forward-path content for distill to capture. The Bernoulli gate let routing collapse to firing_rate=0, which then made every downstream consolidation requirement moot. **The architecture cannot test consolidation with the Bernoulli gate as currently designed, because the gate can decide Local doesn't exist.**

### What would actually test consolidation

For the dispatcher's framing question to be answerable, the next experiment needs to guarantee Local is **active in the forward path during the wake phase**, with non-trivial content, while LR_BANK is still high. The narrowest possible change:

**`MMLLM_LOCAL_FIRING_FLOOR=0.30`** — force Bernoulli gate to fire Local at least 30% of the time, regardless of what its sigmoid output says. This is the dual of the `GATE_W_NET_FLOOR` proposal in earlier journals; same shape, different tier. With Local guaranteed-active:

1. Local's forward output is non-zero → `(local_out − sdpa_out)` has substance → distill target is meaningful.
2. Local accumulates content under high LR_BANK → Δ_local grows during wake.
3. As LR_BANK cools and LR_NET ramps, the meaningful distill target pulls Net's V toward Local's unique contribution.
4. If consolidation works, Δ_net should rise across the back half while Δ_local plateaus or drops — the exact signature the coordinator asked about.

**Telemetry that would prove migration happened in such a run:**
- distill_loss decays across the back half (not grows like here).
- ‖net_out‖ per layer rotates direction toward Local's residual (would need a new logged field: cosine(net_out, local_out − sdpa_out)).
- Δ_local declines as Δ_net rises with Δ_both stable.
- Gate weights drift in Net's favor over the back half as Net's quality matches Local's.

**The harsh honest read:** none of the 9 rounds run so far have actually tested consolidation. Each one either had Local destabilized into noise (round-8 / round-10-3), or had Local accumulating without a meaningful distill target (round-7 / round-8 with target=local), or had the Bernoulli gate disable Local entirely (round-9 v3 / round-10). The architecture's central mechanism — wake-Local → cool-LR_BANK → ramp-LR_NET → distill-transfer → Net-takes-over — has not yet been on the bench with all four ingredients simultaneously working. The pipeline keeps failing at requirement 1 (Local active) or requirement 3 (target meaningful).

That's the gap. The next single experiment that would actually answer the consolidation question is a `LOCAL_FIRING_FLOOR` run on the round-10 LR_BANK=10 or 20 setpoint, with logging that explicitly tracks `cos(net_out, local_residual)` per layer per ablation. If migration happens, the cosine will rise from ~0 to ~1 across the back half. If it doesn't rise, the consolidation hypothesis is falsified, and the architectural story needs revision rather than another tuning sweep.
