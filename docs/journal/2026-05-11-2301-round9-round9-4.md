### Gap analysis

#### What the architectural fix solved

1. **Both failure modes from the prior re-run.** No early spike, no late explosion. 20-event clean run.
2. **The "Net = Local twin" basin** (the round-8 finding). Residual target + direction-only gives Net a unique signal; the basin is gone.
3. **The cycle-decay shape.** Δ_net never decays. The Δ_both trajectory is the steadiest of any round.

#### What the architectural fix did NOT solve

**Net is silent in 3 of 4 layers.** Identical finding to round9-1. L1/L2/L3 have w_net ∈ {0.044, 0.002, 0.006} at end-of-run — gate has routed Net to ~zero everywhere except L0. The brief's "Δ_net sustained > 0.10" target wasn't met for this exact reason — Net's L0-only contribution caps out around 0.099, which is what we observe.

Two workers (round9-1 search_info, round9-4 ops) on two different family slices both converge to **L0=Net-only, L1=Local-only, L2=sdpa-only, L3=Local-only** layout. This is the architecture's preferred equilibrium under the current gate + distill design. The mechanism is the one round9-1 articulates: the 3-way gate softmax can drive w_net to 0 with no penalty, and once w_net=0 in a layer, there's no gradient to Net's V there, and ‖net_out‖ collapses.

#### Single-knob fix to test next

Concurring with round9-1's proposal: **`MMLLM_GATE_W_NET_FLOOR=0.05`**. Post-softmax floor on w_net per layer. Forces every layer to route at least 5% through Net, which (a) keeps gradient flowing to Net's V in L1/L2/L3, (b) prevents Net's per-layer output norm from collapsing to noise, and (c) gives Δ_net headroom above 0.10 by mechanically requiring all four layers to use Net.

Telemetry that would confirm:
- ‖net_out‖ in L1/L2/L3 stays > 5 instead of collapsing to <2.
- Δ_net rises above 0.10 sustained.
- `local_firing_rate` in L1/L3 should be largely unaffected (the floor takes weight from sdpa primarily, not from Local) — that's the side-effect to watch for. If L1/L3 Local firing drops below ~0.3, the floor is squeezing Local too hard and 0.05 should drop to 0.02.

#### One additional knob worth touching (mine, not round9-1's)

The Δ_local floor at +0.027 throughout is interesting. **Local's contribution is non-zero from event 1 and never below 0.027.** This means Local's V was inheriting useful state from the round-6 core resume, not building from scratch. If the next round drops `MMLLM_NET_V_WARMSTART_FROM_LOCAL=false` (currently set in scripts) and tries to also resume Local's V from the round-6 core directly, both bank tiers would start "hot" from the FedAvg merge. That might raise the Δ_local floor further (currently 0.027) and pull Δ_both above 0.20 without touching the gate.
