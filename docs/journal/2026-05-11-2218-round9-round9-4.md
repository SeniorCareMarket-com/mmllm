## Narrative

### Outcomes

**Δ_net broke monotonic decay for the first time.** Full per-ablation trajectory:

```
step:  250  500  750 1000 1250 1500 1750 2000 2250 2500 2750 3000 3250 3500 3750 4000 4250 4500 4750 5000
Δ_net: .020 .041 .081 .050 .033 .048 .032 .036 .062 .040 .061 .059 .044 .293 .306 .222 .231 .245 .258 .282
                       └────── oscillating [.03, .08] band ──────┘ └────── phase shift to [.22, .31] band ──────┘
```

Two phases:
1. **Steps 250–3250: noisy oscillation in [0.02, 0.08]**, looks like the same redundancy basin as round-8 but with a higher noise floor.
2. **Steps 3500–5000: phase shift up to a [0.22, 0.31] band, sustained to end of run.** End-of-run Δ_net = **+0.282**, the largest sleep-cycle-free Δ_net of any worker since round-5.

This is not a one-event spike — the phase shift sustains across the last 7 ablation events. Round-9 worker-1's narrative noted "cross-cycle decay persists" — for the `ops` family that's true through step 3250, then breaks.

**distill_loss sustained huge:** 100 router-metrics samples, range 1.62–4.00, mean **2.33**. Round-8's floor was 5e-4 — this is ~5000× larger. The residual target `(local − sdpa).detach()` produces a much larger MSE signal than the full-local target because Net does NOT match it (Net is carrying information genuinely orthogonal to `local − sdpa`). The fact that distill stays this high throughout training tells you Net is *not* collapsing into the target — it's resisting the pull and learning its own thing. That's the desired behavior.

**consolidation_idx: positive in 19 of 20 ablations** (the one zero is the step-250 baseline by construction). Range +0.60 to +12.37. Round-8 hit +0 to +1; round-9 ops is **10× larger** at peak. The cerebellum's role-take-over signal is unambiguous here.

**fim_eval_bpc: 8.281** (vs round-6 baseline 5.804) — **regressed at the worker level.** Per-language breakdown: json 5.62 (vs 3.79), generic 11.01 (vs 6.58), python 13.59 (vs 9.40), clojure 11.09 (vs 8.43). The JSON regression is the smallest in absolute terms; non-JSON languages took the biggest hits, consistent with `ops`-family overfit eating non-JSON generalization.

**agent_format_validity: 0.000** (vs round-6 baseline 0.110) — regressed. Same pattern as round-7/8 individual workers: narrow shards overfit and lose the held-out structural-validity signal. Merge of 5 family workers is the only test that could recover this.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L0  | 0.715 | 0.000 | 0.285 | 0.321 | 0.00  |  7.51 | 22.31 |
| L1  | 0.750 | 0.000 | 0.250 | 0.752 | 0.12  |  9.42 | 29.67 |

The picture is **per-layer specialization**:
- **L0**: SDPA-dominant trunk (72%), Net carries the remaining 29% with suppressed α (0.32).
- **L1**: SDPA-dominant (75%), Net runs at full α (0.75) for its 25% share — clean two-tier division.
- **L2**: **Fully SDPA, both banks routed to zero.** Net's bank exists but its gate weight is 0. The "dead Net" pattern that round-7/8 saw on some layers — but here only L2 has it, not the whole model.
- **L3**: **Inverted layer.** Net's gate weight is 0 but α_net = 1.155 (the only layer where α > 1) and ‖net_out‖ = 41 (largest in the model). Local is the routed tier here (gw_local = 0.5) and SDPA carries the other half. Net is *not* being routed in the 3-way softmax but it's still active via the Bernoulli gate's "Net-always-runs" branch.

This is not the kind of state any prior round produced. Each layer found a different equilibrium under the residual-distill pressure.

### Gap analysis

**What the residual target did fix.** The "Net = Local twin" basin from round-5 through round-8. Distill MSE was 2.33 throughout this run; in round-8 it was 5e-4. The 5000× larger gradient signal means the optimizer is actively pushing Net *away* from Local — and the late-training Δ_net = +0.28 confirms Net IS carrying unique content that Local can't reconstruct.

**What it did not fix.**

1. **fim_eval and agent_format_validity still regress at the worker level.** The merge across 5 family workers is the only way to recover this. If the merge doesn't beat round-6 5.804 / 0.110, the round-9 hypothesis isn't fully supported — Δ_net would be a within-worker improvement that doesn't survive averaging.

2. **The phase shift at step 3500 is mechanically unexplained.** The trajectory plateau-then-jump is suspicious. Possible causes: (a) the LR_NET cosine 0.5→5.0 crossed a threshold around step 3500 where Net's V started getting big enough updates to matter; (b) L2's gate fully transitioned to SDPA around step 2750, redistributing the gate-weight budget to L0/L1/L3, which then accumulated Net contribution until visibility crossed an ablation threshold; (c) numerical: alpha_net values crossed unity in L3 around then, causing amplified output that ablation now sees. Telemetry doesn't disambiguate; instrumenting `net_v_grad_norm` over time would.

3. **L3 is doing something the gating ontology doesn't describe.** gw_net = 0 but α_net = 1.155 and ‖net_out‖ = 41 (largest in the model). The Bernoulli gate is letting Net run even when the 3-way softmax routes 0% to it, and α_net is amplifying the result. The ablation Δ_net = +0.28 likely comes mostly from L3's "shadow contribution." Whether this is a bug or an emergent compensation depends on whether L3's Net is consistent across batches or noise. Worth a deep-dive.

4. **L2 going fully SDPA at step 2750+ might be a routing pathology the Bernoulli gate is *causing*.** When Local is Bernoulli-gated, the trunk gets stochastic Local input — that's noisy. SDPA is deterministic. The optimizer at L2 chose deterministic SDPA over noisy Local AND over Net (which has its own distill-driven pull away from local). Whether L2's "dead Net" is recoverable in deeper training or whether it's a stable local optimum is the next question.

5. **Δ_local is barely above noise throughout** (max +0.0039). Local's bank exists, K_a/K_b carry the round-6 routing geometry, V is fresh-init, but the model has no use for it. The Bernoulli gate may have crossed a threshold where Local is functionally severed from the gradient flow except via the rare invocations.

**Is Δ_net's late-training rise real consolidation or saturation against a static target?**

I think **late-emerging real consolidation**, not saturation:
- If saturation: Δ_net would plateau, but instead it climbs 0.22 → 0.31 → 0.22 → 0.23 → 0.25 → 0.26 → 0.28 — slow upward drift across the phase-2 band, not flat saturation.
- The `(local − sdpa)` target is itself slowly changing as Local's V re-fills from random (it's reset at training start because warm-start is suppressed). So the distill target moves, Net keeps adapting.
- consolidation_idx keeps growing (peaks at 12.4 at step 3750), then settles to 5. That's not the signature of a saturated static target; it's the signature of Net's relative importance *still increasing* relative to Local even after the phase shift.

**Bug-looking telemetry observation.** L3 has gw_net = 0 in the 3-way softmax but α_net = 1.155 and ‖net_out‖ = 41. The block code applies α_net to `net_out` BEFORE the gate softmax mixes (per gating.py line ~135). So `α_net · net_out` is what gets weighted by `w2 = 0` and contributes nothing to the 3-way mix. But the Bernoulli-gate branch (`MMLLM_GATE_NET_DEFAULT=true`) might be re-injecting `α_net · net_out` outside the 3-way path. If so, the per-head `α_net` is doing real work in L3 even though the softmax says it shouldn't. Worth a code inspection to confirm whether `α_net` is double-applied or not.

### One concrete next experiment

**Hypothesis to test:** the late-training Δ_net rise is driven by a single layer (L3) via the Bernoulli-gate's "Net-always-runs" path, not by the residual-distill teaching the other layers something Local doesn't have.

**Knob:** add per-layer Δ_net ablation events — zero `Net.V` for each layer individually and measure the val_bpc impact per layer. (Current ablation zeroes all 4 layers' Net V at once.)

**Specific telemetry that would tell us if it worked:** if zeroing L3's Net V alone produces 80%+ of the total Δ_net = +0.28, then the round-9 win is one-layer-driven and probably an emergent property of the Bernoulli + α_net interaction at L3, NOT a generic residual-distill success. If the Δ_net is roughly evenly distributed across L0/L1/L2/L3 (each contributes ~+0.07), then residual-distill is doing what it claims at the model level.

This is a 30-second instrumentation change in core.lpy (four extra ablation calls per ablation event) and would unambiguously identify whether the round-9 result generalizes or is a single-layer phenomenon.
