## Narrative

### Headline

**Hypothesis refuted on every Net-side metric.** distill at coef=0.1 with the
default MSE form did not drive Local→Net transfer. Post-sleep `Δ_net` is
**monotonically declining** across all 5 cycles (0.0074 → 0.0053 → 0.0047 →
0.0019 → 0.0013), the opposite of the predicted "rising across cycles" signal.
**FIM-bpc regressed 5.804 → 7.653** vs the round-6 baseline, and
**format_validity collapsed from 0.110 to 0.000**. Three of four headline
targets failed.

| metric | target | round-6 baseline | round8-1 | hit? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `val_bpc_final` | < 0.32 | 0.272 | **0.2299** | ✅ |
| post-sleep Δ_net rising | rising/holding | — | **monotonic decline** | ❌ |
| `fim_eval_bpc` | ≤ 5.804 | 5.804 | **7.653** | ❌ |
| `format_validity` | ≥ 0.110 | 0.110 | **0.000** | ❌ |

### `distill_loss` trajectory

100 samples logged from `router-metrics`. Distill ramps down from
**0.0070 → 0.0005 within the first 250 steps**, then floors at a steady
**~5e-4 for the rest of the run** (last 5: 0.0005, 0.0005, 0.0005, 0.0006,
0.0005). At `MMLLM_DISTILL_COEF=0.1` the effective gradient pressure on Net
is `0.1 × 5e-4 = 5e-5` — three orders of magnitude under the main loss
(~0.15-0.2). Net got essentially no distillation signal after step 250.

This is **the same magnitude regime as round-4** (~3e-4, direction-only),
which the round-5 dispatch flagged as the redundancy-basin bug.
Magnitude-aware distill was supposed to push this to **~2e-3**, ~4× higher
than what landed here. Either `MMLLM_DISTILL_DIRECTION_ONLY` is still
defaulting to `true` in this dispatch, or the magnitude term collapsed
again under the round-6-inherited NetBank V (which was already low-norm
from FedAvg averaging).

A naive "is the post-sleep peak rising" read says: cycles 2 and 3 did
produce a post-sleep bump, but the absolute levels are all in the noise
range (~5e-3 = 0.05% of the dense control bpc). The trajectory across
cycles is **declining**, not rising — exactly the same pattern round-7
showed without distill. Distill at this magnitude is invisible at the
Δ_net measurement level.

### Did Local→Net transfer occur?

**No, at the measurement-floor.** Three independent signals agree:

1. distill_loss floored at ~5e-4 (same as round-4 direction-only failure mode).
2. Post-sleep Δ_net never exceeded its first-cycle level; cross-cycle
   trend was monotonic decline.
3. `fim_eval_bpc` regressed by 32% vs round-6 baseline and
   `format_validity` collapsed to zero. If Net were absorbing Local's
   accumulated content, we'd expect at least preservation of the
   format-validity ability that lived in the round-6 NetBank V; instead
   we lost it entirely.

### Why fim_eval / format_validity regressed below the resume baseline

The dispatch resumed from a checkpoint that already scored
fim_eval=5.804, format_validity=0.110. Training for 5k steps at
LR_DENSE=0.5×, LR_BANK=10×, LR_NET=0.5× on a fresh single-shard corpus
**overwrote** that capability:

- Dense at 0.5× peak (1.5e-3 effective) still has enough plasticity to
  drift on 5k batches of a JSON-only distribution.
- Local at 10× peak (3e-2) hill-climbs aggressively on its single shard
  — `val_bpc` dropped to 0.23, lowest of any worker so far.
- Net at 0.5× peak with distill_loss=5e-4 has neither the LR nor the
  signal to defend the inherited content; it just decays.

So per-token autoregressive bpc on the shard improves while the
multi-language structural-completion task (fim-eval at clojure/generic/
python) and the OOD format-emission task (agent-eval) both collapse.
Classic over-fit to the shard, with the cerebellum's pre-loaded knowledge
sloshed out.

### What this argues for round-9

The single-question framing — "did Local→Net transfer occur?" — was
answered no, but the **mechanism** failure mode points at distill
magnitude, not the consolidation hypothesis itself. The constructive
next moves, in order of cost:

1. **Verify `MMLLM_DISTILL_DIRECTION_ONLY=false`** is actually being
   honored. Round-5's deep-dive found this knob silently inverted before;
   the ~5e-4 floor here is suspiciously identical to that bug's regime.
   Single env-var print at startup would settle it.
2. **Raise `MMLLM_DISTILL_COEF` to 1.0** (10× this run). Even at the
   measured magnitude floor 5e-4, that gives 5e-4 effective per-step
   contribution, comparable to a meaningful regularizer.
3. **Cosine LR_NET 0.5→5×** across the run (P1 schedule). Net at fixed
   0.5× starves regardless of distill — the schedule was designed
   to crossover Local LR_BANK mid-run so Net inherits the content as
   Local cools. We're running flat lr_n=1.5e-3 the whole time.
4. **Reduce LR_DENSE further** (0.1× or freeze after step 1000). Dense
   at 0.5× absorbs everything before the bank tier can; that's the
   round-6a/round-7-1 collapse mode plus distill noise.

The "post-sleep Δ_net rose +39% in cycle 2, +24% in cycle 3" reading
is the only encouraging thread — at higher distill coef the same
mechanism might hold across all 5 cycles. Worth one more round before
declaring the consolidation hypothesis dead.
