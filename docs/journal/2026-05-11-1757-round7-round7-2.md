## Hypothesis
Each shard's worker consolidates a non-overlapping slice of the tool-call
distribution into its NetBank. Harvest (FedAvg or distillation) then has
genuinely complementary content to combine, not 5 near-redundant generalists.

Target: post-harvest fim_eval < 5.5 OR format_validity > 0.150 — beating
round-6's headline by a non-trivial margin.

### Why the magnitude is half of round-6's

We resumed from round-6's community core, which already has a much tighter
ctrl bpc baseline (0.33 at step 250 vs round-6's 0.86). The bank tier's
absolute Δ_net contribution is bounded by *what's left for it to do
beyond dense+sdpa*, and after the round-6 harvest there's substantially
less left. Same architectural mechanism as round-6 (post-sleep Δ_net does
briefly rise after every sleep), just operating in a tighter bpc regime
where the differential is necessarily smaller.

The watch-list assertion "Δ_local should stay near 0 between sleeps" held
exactly: Δ_local was negative or sub-±0.0014 at every measurement, and the
sleep mechanism stayed non-destructive (ctrl held under 0.6 except for the
one transient at step 4500).

**The hash-by-doc-id partition produces 5 statistically near-identical
slices**, not 5 specializations. xlam-synth has ~20 tool types uniformly
distributed across docs, so `id mod 5` randomly picks 20% of each tool
without favoring any particular tool family in any shard. Shard 2's docs
look like the corpus average to within ±10%.

This means **round-7's "workers see different content" premise doesn't
fully hold for this corpus.** Workers see different *random samples*
(different noise) but not different *content* (no real specialization).
The hypothesis-as-stated — that sharding produces complementary
specialists whose merge benefit grows beyond round-6's same-corpus
ceiling — has only partial purchase here. Any harvest gain would be
ensemble noise-averaging, not content composition.

### Read on whether the harvest will combine constructively

Best case: averaging 5 models trained on near-identical (just differently-
sampled) data acts as a noise-averaging ensemble that recovers the
round-6 core's format_validity (~0.110) and slightly improves its
fim_eval — the standard FedAvg-on-IID-data win.

Realistic case: ~match round-6's headline. With each individual at 0.000
format_validity (regressed from 0.110), the merge can't pull above
0.110 without genuine content divergence. Round-7 likely demonstrates
that **sharding by hash on a uniformly-distributed-tool corpus is a
null intervention** — same takeaway as round-6's "distillation matched
FedAvg because content was identical."

The cleanest next experiment to validate this read: **partition by
tool family, not by doc id**. Shard 0 = `search_web` + `get_*` (lookup
tools); shard 1 = `post_message` + `send_email` (write tools); etc.
Each worker trains primarily on one tool *family*. That'd produce
genuinely different NetBank specializations and would test whether
the architecture composes complementary content vs just noise-averages.
