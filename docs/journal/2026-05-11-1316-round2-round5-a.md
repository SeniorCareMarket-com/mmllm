## Narrative

**Headline read**: 3 of 5 round-5 targets hit. The two upstream fixes
(sleep-cycle LR actually applied + magnitude-aware distill) deliver
exactly what the deep-dive predicted on the *mechanism* side: `‖net‖/‖local‖`
hits ≈1.0 across all layers, `alpha_net` stays meaningfully positive,
distill loss is ~10× round-4. **But `Δ_net` still pins at the noise
floor at end-of-run (+0.0004)**, briefly touching the target only at
step 1000 (+0.0096). The dispatch's pre-registered fallback fires:
**residual distill is now the indicated next step**.

### What the two upstream fixes accomplished

**1. Real sleep-cycle LR (was a ghost in r1–r4).** Live param-group
LRs match the printed values now — `lr_b` cosines from 2.99e-02 (step 50)
down to 3.00e-03 (step 5000), `lr_n` rises 3.01e-03 → 3.00e-02. Crossover
land at step 2500 (`lr_b = lr_n = 1.65e-02`) exactly as designed. The
trajectory is what r1–r4 *thought* they were running.

**2. Magnitude-aware distill (was direction-only in r4).** Step-1000
telemetry showed the magnitude fix is doing exactly what the deep-dive
predicted:

| Layer | step 1000 ‖net‖ | ‖local‖ | ratio | r4 same ratio |
|-------|-----------------|---------|-------|---------------|
| 0 | 6.42 | 8.11 | **0.79** | 0.08 |
| 1 | 3.26 | 3.25 | **1.00** | 0.15 |
| 2 | 6.72 | 6.78 | **0.99** | 0.20 |
| 3 | 2.29 | 2.22 | **1.03** | 0.21 |

5-10× larger relative magnitude in every layer. By step 5000, ratios
stay 0.98–1.00. NetBank's V is genuinely growing magnitude.

**3. alpha_net no longer bifurcates.** Round-4 had two heads in L3 at
0.006 (deep-dive flagged this as "per-head dampening loophole"). Round-5
L3 alpha_net = 0.42, all four layers keep alpha_net_mean > 0.20.
Magnitude-aware distill closed the alpha-collapse path.

**4. Synergy is consistently large.** Δ_both − (Δ_local + Δ_net):
+0.87, +1.13, +0.78, +0.52, +0.42 across the 5 ablation events. Far
larger than r4's +0.15. NetBank IS carrying complementary signal that
shows up only when Local is also removed.

### Why Δ_net still pins at noise floor

This is the round-4 deep-dive prediction playing out. The architecture
has a **redundancy basin**: distill (any form) forces Net's output toward
Local's direction. With magnitude now matched, Net is now a true twin
of Local — same direction, same magnitude. The optimizer's response is
unchanged: when given two functionally redundant tiers, suppress one.

Round-4 used two suppression paths:
- per-head alpha_net → 0 (forbidden in r5 by magnitude-aware distill)
- *not used much* — gate routing was the main path

Round-5 with the alpha path closed found the **gate-routing path** is
fully sufficient on its own. Watch mean `gate_w_net` shrink across
the run:

| step | mean gate_w_net | L0 | L1 | L2 | L3 |
|------|-----------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 1000 | 0.200 | 0.20 | 0.23 | 0.00 | 0.36 |
| 2000 | 0.213 | 0.22 | 0.24 | 0.01 | 0.39 |
| 5000 | **0.149** | 0.16 | 0.04 | 0.01 | 0.39 |

L1 collapsed gate_w_net from 0.23 → 0.04 across the run. L2 was always
near 0. The model decided: "Net's output has the right magnitude and
direction, but it's saying the same thing as Local, so route to Local
instead." Same outcome as r4, different mechanism.

The **deep-dive's redundancy hypothesis is now confirmed**. Closing
one suppression path doesn't help — the optimizer finds another path
to the same equilibrium.

### Per-head gate_w_local sanity check

At step 5000 L0, gate_w_local = 0.66 (Local is the dominant route).
L1: 0.21. L2: 0.01. L3: 0.00. So **the model uses Local heavily in L0
and ignores it in L2/L3**, choosing sdpa instead. Both Local *and* Net
get suppressed in L2/L3 — sdpa carries those layers.

### Δ_net trajectory: brief target hit at step 1000, then collapse

```
step:   1000    2000    3000    4000    5000
Δ_net: +0.0096 -0.0012 +0.0026 +0.0034 +0.0004
```

Step 1000 is the only point above the +0.01 threshold (barely).
That's during the warmup phase when **lr_n is still small (~3e-3)** —
Net's V hasn't yet been heavily updated, distill loss is at its
highest (0.0044), so Net's output still carries some unique
information that distill is forcing toward Local. After lr_n rises
through the crossover (step 2500), Net's V gets bigger CE-loss
gradients on top of the distill pressure → V converges harder to
Local's role → redundancy completes → Δ_net collapses.

Counterintuitively: **NetBank gets more redundant the more it's
trained at high LR**. The sleep cycle is doing its job — but it's
doing exactly the wrong thing for breaking redundancy.

### What round 5 demonstrated

The dispatch's hypothesis was: "with sleep-cycle LR actually firing AND
magnitude-aware distill, Δ_net should escape the noise floor for the
first time." Round 5 confirms that **the architectural plumbing is
correct**:

- Resume actually loads core/round-2 (val_bpc starts at ~0.4 vs r3's
  diverged-from-random)
- LR rotation actually applies to param_groups (verified in lr_n /
  lr_b printouts which now reflect the optimizer's real state)
- Distillation pressure is now magnitude-aware (~3× the raw MSE)
- NetBank V fp16 persists in the worker payload (file sizes: 64MB ×
  4 layers, well under GitHub's 100MB limit)
- gate, alpha_net, and ‖net‖ all "look healthy" by every metric

But **Δ_net didn't escape the noise floor**. The deep-dive's prediction:
that even with magnitude fixed, the redundancy basin would re-emerge
via a different suppression path. Confirmed by gate_w_net trajectory.

### Recommendation for round 6: residual distill

The pre-registered fallback should fire. The fix is small (3-line
edit in `collect-distill-loss`):

```clojure
;; current (magnitude-aware):
(F/mse_loss no (.detach lo))

;; round-6 (residual distill):
(F/mse_loss no (.sub (.detach lo) (.detach no)))   ; Net learns Local - Net
;; or even cleaner:
;; target = Local - alpha · (running stop-gradient of Net)
```

The intuition: instead of teaching Net to copy Local's direction
(redundant role), teach Net to predict Local's residual — what Local
*didn't capture* this step. Net then becomes a non-redundant boost
on top of Local, and any suppression of Net leaves a hole that
Local can't fill (because Local already predicted its own contribution
when Net was active during training).

Alternative formulations to consider in round 6:
1. **Predict residual against last-N-steps stale Local**: `target =
   Local_current - Local_stale_500_steps_ago`. Forces Net to capture
   "new" learning.
2. **Inverse distill at high-loss positions**: distill only fires when
   per-token CE loss > τ. Net specializes in hard examples.
3. **Adversarial gate**: gate routes through Net at training time but
   not at inference, with an L1 penalty on `gate_w_local` for those
   positions. Forces Net to learn what Local can't.
