### Post-sleep Δ_net per cycle — the answer to "did Local→Net transfer occur?"

```
post-sleep #1 (step 1250):  Δ_net = +0.0122  ↑
post-sleep #2 (step 2250):  Δ_net = +0.0052  ↓
post-sleep #3 (step 3250):  Δ_net = +0.0056  ↓ (~flat)
post-sleep #4 (step 4250):  Δ_net = +0.0036  ↓
```

The trajectory has a cycle-1 spike (post-sleep > pre-sleep by 0.005)
followed by decline across cycles 2–4. The hypothesis predicted
*Local's wake-time accumulation moving into Net*, which should manifest
as a *rising* post-sleep Δ_net trend as more cumulative content lands
in NetBank. We observe the opposite: cycle-1 produces the largest
delta and subsequent cycles plateau or decline.

### Why didn't the transfer accumulate?

The NetBank telemetry tells the story. Across the run:

- `w_local` collapses from 0.148 → 0.008 — the 3-way gate stops
  routing through Local almost entirely. By step 1250 (one cycle in),
  `w_local` is already at 0.023.
- `w_net` rises 0.255 → 0.439 (~1.7×) — Net takes Local's share of
  the gate.
- `L_out` and `N_out` both grow in absolute norm (Local 1.86 → 3.06,
  Net 1.90 → 2.81), so the V tensors are accumulating mass on both
  paths, but Local's contribution is gated almost to zero.
- `alpha_net` continues its anti-overshoot decay (0.53 → 0.38).

With `w_local ≈ 0.01`, the **distill loss** `MSE(net, local.detach())`
is operating on a Local signal the gate barely uses to begin with.
LR_BANK=10× pushes Local's V to a large norm fast, but the model
discovers it can ignore Local at the gate level — and at that point,
distill is teaching Net to mimic a tier that contributes ~1% of the
attention output. The "Local accumulates → distill transfers → sleep
wipes → repeat" pump never engages because Local's gate weight
collapses before the pump's second iteration.

This is the *gate-side* analog of the round-5 redundancy basin: in
round-5 distill drove Net to mirror Local's direction; in round-8 the
gate routes around Local so distill has nothing of value to transfer.
The model finds a stable solution that bypasses the consolidation
mechanism entirely.

### Observability gap

`distill_loss` is not recorded as a per-step telemetry field in
`log.jsonl` (only `distill_coef=0.1` appears, in meta.json). To
diagnose this kind of failure mode in future rounds, the train loop
should log `distill_loss_mean` per ablation event alongside
`gate_w_*` and the per-layer norms. Without it, "did distill engage?"
has to be inferred from `w_local` collapse and the post-sleep Δ_net
trajectory, which is a noisier signal.

### val_bpc and out-of-distribution

val_bpc 0.2174 is well under the 0.32 target — and below round-7
shard-3's 0.258 despite training on the full corpus (no sharding).
Distill + LR_BANK=10× does help in-distribution. fim_eval 7.681
is worse than round-7 shard-3's 7.281 and round-6 FedAvg's 5.804;
the per-language breakdown (clojure 11.91, generic 10.94, json 4.51,
python 12.05) tracks the same specialization-as-overfit pattern
round-7 showed, just slightly more pronounced. Agent eval is at
zeros (expected pre-harvest for an individual worker at this scale).

### Harvest expectation

The hypothesis under test — "distill produces a rising Δ_net trend
because each wake accumulates into Local and each sleep transfers
the accumulation into Net" — is not confirmed by this worker. What
the harvest will likely show:

1. **fim_eval probably worse than round-6's 5.804.** Five workers
   that all collapsed `w_local` to ~0.01 by step 1250 are five
   workers with similar gate routings. The FedAvg of their dense
   trunks may average past the individual overfits and recover
   ground, but the underlying consolidation mechanism didn't engage.
2. **format_validity probably near 0.110 or below.** Round-6 already
   captured the gain available from FedAvg-of-5-same-corpus. Round-8
   added distill + LR_BANK=10× hoping to push past that ceiling via
   a working consolidation pump; if the pump didn't engage, the
   harvest won't exceed round-6's headline.

The cleanest next experimental lever: **find the LR_BANK / LR_NET
ratio where `w_local` does NOT collapse**. Either reduce LR_BANK
(let Local accumulate slowly enough that the gate doesn't have time
to route around it) or add an entropy regularizer on the 3-way gate
softmax (penalize collapsed routing) or use a per-tier KL warm-up.
Once `w_local` stays bounded away from zero, the distill pump has
something to actually transfer, and the post-sleep Δ_net trend
becomes a meaningful diagnostic again.

### Bottom line

Round-8 confirms sleep cycles work (Δ_local pinned in [-0.0012,
+0.0006], ctrl_bpc descends to 0.217), confirms distill is config-
plumbed (coef=0.1 in meta.json, fim_eval shape consistent with
distill being applied), but **does not confirm that the
consolidation pump engages**. The gate's collapse of `w_local`
makes the distill transfer no-op-equivalent for cycles 2–5. The
mechanism needs gate-side stabilization before "post-sleep Δ_net
rises across cycles" can be tested as a meaningful diagnostic.
