collapsed by **10×** in the first ~250 steps and stayed there:

                                 Local's V is random init; the divergence

The pipeline is working as designed. Net DOES learn to mimic Local's

brief spike to 0.0023-0.0028 (Local re-accumulating from zero, MSE
gap reopens) that recovered within ~3 reporting intervals.

## Agent eval

```
→ format=0.000  name=0.000  args=0.000  exact=0.000  (297.3s)
```

**Regressed from round-6 core's 0.110 → 0.000**, same shape as round-7.

This is the clearest signal of the round: **single-worker retraining
loses the merged-core's format-validity property**, regardless of
whether the consolidation pipeline is on (round-8) or off (round-7).
The 0.110 format_validity was a property of the FedAvg ensemble across
5 same-corpus workers — averaging 5 noisy 0.000-or-0.020 individuals
into a 0.110 was the win. Re-training any individual on top of that
average for 5000 steps moves the dense weights far enough from the
average that the property dissolves.

## Diagnosis: pipeline works, content doesn't help

The two findings together:

1. **The consolidation pipeline (distill + replay + sleep cycle) is
   mechanically functional.** distill_loss collapses, post-sleep Δ_net
   survives 3 sleeps instead of 1-2 (round-6/7), val_bpc reaches a new
   floor at 0.232.

2. **But the *content* Net is mirroring is not useful for the held-out
   evals.** Net becomes a parallel copy of Local; Local is over-fitting
   the JSON FIM corpus; FIM-eval and format_validity regress.

The round-9 implication: **transfer mechanism + actually-diverse content
are both necessary**. Round-7 had divergent samples but no transfer.
Round-8 had transfer but only one corpus slice. The cleanest next
experiment is **multi-worker (sharded) AND distill-enabled**:

- Workers 0..4 each train on a tool-family-partitioned shard
  (not hash-by-doc-id; round-7 showed hash gives near-identical slices).
- Each worker runs the round-8 consolidation pipeline (distill ON).
- Harvest dense via FedAvg, NetBank via distill-from-real-queries.

That tests whether genuine content divergence + functional consolidation
compose into a merged core that beats round-6's 5.804 / 0.110.

## What I'd flag for the coordinator

The Δ_net "hold across 3 sleeps then collapse on 4" suggests something
specific is happening between sleep #3 and #4. Both ctrl_bpc and Δ_both
crossed inflection points at step ~4000 — possibly Adam moments
converging tighter, or replay buffer dominance increasing as Local's
training set effectively shrinks. Worth a finer-grained ablation
(e.g., MMLLM_ABLATE_EVERY=100) between steps 3500-4500 in a future
round if cross-cycle holding is a target.
