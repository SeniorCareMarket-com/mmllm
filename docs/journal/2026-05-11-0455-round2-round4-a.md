### What the new mechanics demonstrated

The round-3 bugs (SumGate vs SwitchGate; step-0 vs step-1 resume) are
genuinely fixed in this dispatch — and the diagnostics are now richly
visible:

**1. Resume from round-2 core fired.** Log says
`train-long: steps=1→5000`, confirming `latest-checkpoint-step` saw
step-1 and triggered `load-checkpoint!`. 66/74 dense tensors loaded
(8 skipped due to topology change — alpha_net + gate_proj_3 etc. are
new params that didn't exist in the round-2 core).

**2. NetBank V warm-start fired with real content.** Logs show
`NetBank[layer N] warm-started from Local K_a/K_b + V (projected)`
across all 4 layers. Unlike round 3, this time the projection had
a meaningfully-loaded Local-Bank-equivalent shape to start from
(the dense.pt loaded gave the model a working starting point).

**3. SwitchGate is doing 3-way routing.** Per-layer telemetry from

### Why Δ_net is still ~0 despite gate_w_net > 0.30

The mechanism's wired but the **magnitude is still small**. NetBank's
`net_out_norm` is 0.55-0.84 versus Local's `local_out_norm` 2.7-10.2 —
NetBank's contribution is ~5-10% of Local's by L2 norm. When you zero
NetBank V in ablation, the SwitchGate still weights NetBank's output by
gate_w_net ~0.30, but that output is just a tiny vector relative to the
other tiers. The signal that disappears is real but tiny.

`alpha_net` is _decreasing_ across the run (e.g. L3: 0.60 → 0.26)
suggesting the model is actively learning to _dampen_ NetBank's output
magnitude — which is consistent with NetBank not having developed
distinctive content yet that the model wants to amplify.

The other side of the same coin: **Δ_local kept growing** through the
run (+1.66 → +2.07 → +2.56 → +3.22 → +2.78), and `synergy = Δ_both -
Δ_local - Δ_net` grew from +0.15 to +0.28. Local consolidated the
training signal hard, NetBank picked up complementary signal that
shows up in synergy but not in standalone ablation.

### Symptoms worth flagging

- **FIM-bpc OVERALL regressed (3.58 → 7.06).** Within-distribution
  val_bpc dropped to 0.289 (best ever) but the cross-language
  FIM eval rows all regressed: clojure 4.65 → 10.39, python 4.39 →
  10.16, generic 3.33 → 9.93. The diversified synth corpus + heavy
  consolidation knobs (distill + replay + 10× Local LR) seem to
  produce a model that's _more_ specialized to the training
  distribution and _less_ transferable. Memorization hypothesis: the
  replay buffer pulls "easy" positions repeatedly, reinforcing
  distribution-specific bytes.
- **`net_v_grad_norm` is uneven across layers.** L0 gets 7.8e-4,
  L1-L3 get 5e-6 to 1e-4. The top layer eats most of the NetBank
  gradient signal; the deeper layers' V barely moves. May indicate
  the consolidation pressure isn't distributing evenly.

### Recommendation for round 5

The 3-way SwitchGate genuinely works — the consolidation _scaffold_
is live. The next missing piece is making NetBank's output magnitude
competitive with Local's. Candidate fixes:

1. **Boost `alpha_net` upward** — initialize it at 2.0 instead of 1.0,
   or add an `MMLLM_ALPHA_NET_MIN` floor. The model is learning to
   shrink it because NetBank's content is initially weak; a higher
   floor would force NetBank to mean something even if it has to
   amplify random noise initially.
2. **Detach the gate gradient from `gate_w_local`** so the model can't
   route _away_ from Local just to avoid the distill loss. Currently
   gate_w_local has gone to ~0 in 3/4 layers — that defeats the
   "Local hill-walks first" half of the sleep cycle.
3. **Bigger NetBank** — `MMLLM_NET_SQRT_N` at 1024 gives ~1M entries.
   Doubling to 2048 (~4M entries) gives more capacity for content
   accumulation without changing the LR.
4. **Longer run** — 5k steps is too short for SparseAdam on a sparse
   bank. 50k+ would let `net_v_grad_norm`-driven updates accumulate
   meaningful mass per row.

### Surprises

- Loss bounced into the 0.1-0.3 range by step 700, vs round 3's
  10+ at that point. The resume bug really was the round-3 killer.
- gate_w_local crashed to ~0 in layers 1-3 by step 1000 already.
  The model very quickly decided "Local isn't useful, route around it"
  — yet Δ_local stays huge (+2.78 end-of-run). Reconciliation: gate
  weights compete softmax-wise, but the bank's _content_ still
  carries the signal that the dense path implicitly depends on
  through the residual stream. Zeroing V breaks what's there, even if
  the gate isn't routing much through Local.
