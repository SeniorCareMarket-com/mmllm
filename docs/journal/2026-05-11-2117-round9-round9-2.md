## Read on the experiment

**Round-9's mechanical wins**: distill is no longer starved, ctrl bpc
hits the tightest value of any round (0.222), Net's first-cycle Δ_net
peak is 3× round-8's. The Bernoulli gate works as designed.

**Where it falls short**: cross-cycle Δ_net still collapses. Net is
getting plenty of transfer signal early, but by sleep #2 Local has
re-accumulated enough that distill pulls Net back toward Local's
*current* state — and as both converge toward what dense+sdpa already
handle, Δ_net tends to zero. The architecture still doesn't produce
*durable* consolidation that survives multiple cycles, only a
one-shot first-cycle transfer.

**Hypothesis for round-10**: the issue isn't the gate or the distill
coefficient; it's that distill is MSE(net_out, local_out.detach()) and
local_out keeps re-converging to whatever dense+sdpa already covers.
Net mimics what Local mimics, and Local re-mimics dense. To get
durable Net consolidation, distill should target something dense can't
reproduce — e.g., a residual `(local_out − sdpa_baseline_out)` so
Net specifically learns what Local *adds beyond sdpa*, not Local's
total output. That's a more substantial mechanism change than this
round's gate swap.

**Format validity regression**: same shape as round-7/8 — single-worker
training on a slice retrains dense away from whatever held the
round-6 core's 0.110 in place. The harvest across 5 workers may
recover it (as round-6 itself demonstrated: best-individual 0.020 →
merged 0.110); if it doesn't, that's evidence that 5 workers training
on disjoint family slices have moved dense too far apart for FedAvg
to reconverge to a format-emitting state.
