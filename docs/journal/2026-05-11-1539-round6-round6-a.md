# Round 6 — Sleep cycle — round6-a

## Mechanism
- sleep cycle: V_local ← 0 every 1000 steps
- distill: OFF (would pull Net→0 with V_local=0)
- replay: OFF (mastery threshold can't fire under repeated Local resets)
- LR: flat — bank=1× base, net=10× base
- ablate cadence: 250 steps (captures pre/post-sleep Δ trajectory)

## Round-5 → 6 baseline
- Round-5a step-5000: Δ_local=3.7601, Δ_net=0.0004, Δ_both=4.1767
- Δ_both − Δ_local = +0.4166 (Net's masked capacity at that ckpt)
- 10-min spike at the same ckpt with V_local zeroed + LR_BANK frozen:
  Δ_net jumped 0.0004 → 0.7381 within 250 steps.
  Δ_net = 0.6486 by step 5750 (steady).

## Setup
- date (UTC): Mon May 11 15:39:39 UTC 2026
- handle: round6-a
- resume from: core/round-2/step-20000/dense.pt
- NetBank: enabled, sqrt_n=1024, c_net=32

## Hypothesis
Each sleep cycle frees Local's V; Net's masked contribution becomes visible
in Δ_net. As cycles repeat, Net densifies further and Δ_net grows. Local
specializes on what Net+sdpa don't cover (because gate routes those
residuals to Local once Net is established). End-of-run target:
Δ_net ≥ 0.5, ideally rising across cycles.

## Narrative

### Headline
**Δ_net did not cross the noise floor in the way the spike predicted.** Across
the 5 sleep events, the post-sleep Δ_net peak rose from 0.098 → 0.116, then
**collapsed** to 0.038 → 0.023. End-of-run Δ_net = +0.016, ~40× under the 0.5
headline target and ~30× under the spike's steady-state 0.65. Hypothesis as
stated is **refuted on this resume** — sleep cycles alone do not produce a
stable, rising consolidation signal at this ckpt.

### Why this differs from the round-5a spike

The spike took round-5a step-5000 — a ckpt with **Δ_local = 3.7601** — zeroed
V_local, and surfaced 0.65–0.74 bpc of previously-masked Net capacity within
250 steps. That worked because Local was carrying a huge reservoir of signal;
removing it exposed everything underneath.

This run resumed from round-2 step-20000 (Net warm-started from that core's
fp16 V) and trained 5k fresh steps at flat LR_BANK=1×. Look at the
pre-sleep Δ_local column: **never above +0.024 anywhere in the run, and
~+0.003 from step 1750 onward**. Local doesn't accumulate enough between
1000-step windows to have anything substantial for sleep to expose. The
mechanism is correct (post-sleep Δ_net does briefly rise after every sleep —
see 0.053→0.098, 0.043→0.116, 0.068→0.038, 0.032→0.023), but the magnitudes
are bounded by how loaded Local was, which here is barely loaded at all.

Meanwhile, dense+sdpa absorbs the work that the bank tier would otherwise
own: **ctrl bpc falls 0.862 → 0.268**, Δ_both falls **0.434 → 0.020**. By
step 4500 the bank tier (Local + Net combined) contributes ~2% of the
predictive signal. The model is collapsing toward dense-only.

### control_bpc behavior

control_bpc held safely under the 0.6 watch threshold from step 750 onward
and ended at **0.268** — far better than 0.6. Net+sdpa cover Local's work
cleanly post-sleep (no degradation visible at any boundary), so the sleep
mechanism itself is non-destructive. The problem isn't that sleep breaks
things; it's that there's nothing to expose.

### Watch-list triage

| symptom                       | observed?            | implication |
|-------------------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| ctrl_bpc > 1.0 persistently   | **no** (max 0.86 at step 250, then <0.5) | interval=1000 was fine here |
| Δ_net oscillating w/o trend   | **yes** (capped ~0.12, no rise) | c_net=32 capacity ceiling, OR Local-reservoir issue (below) |
| Δ_local snaps to 3.7+         | **no** (Δ_local ≤ 0.024 anywhere)        | flat LR_BANK=1× too cold for Local to rebuild |

The "Δ_local stays near zero between sleeps" finding is the real story here.
The post-sleep Δ_net is bounded by whatever Local *would have* contributed
in the next stretch, and at LR_BANK=1× Local barely contributes anything.

### Round-7 candidates, ranked by what this run argues for

1. **Re-run from a Local-loaded ckpt.** Round-5a step-5000 (Δ_local=3.76) is
   the obvious choice. The 10-min spike already proved the mechanism works
   there. Generalize the spike: 5 sleeps × 1000-step intervals, c_net=32
   held fixed (rule out the capacity ceiling as a confounder first).

2. **Post-sleep LR_BANK ramp.** Even without changing resume ckpt, if Local
   were given (say) LR_BANK=10× for the first 250 steps after each sleep
   and then dropped to 1×, Δ_local should rebuild measurably between
   sleeps, and the next post-sleep Δ_net should rise correspondingly. This
   isolates the LR-as-reservoir hypothesis.

3. **c_net=128** (the watch-list's nominal next move). Only worth running
   AFTER #1 or #2 — until Δ_net consistently presses against ~0.5, raising
   c_net is solving a problem the data doesn't yet have.

4. **Dense LR decay.** Dense absorbed ctrl bpc 0.86 → 0.27 in 5k steps,
   leaving the bank tier almost no work. Cosine'ing dense LR down across
   training (or freezing dense after step 1000) would preserve more signal
   in the bank tier and give sleep something to play with.

The cheapest unambiguous next move is **#1 — repeat the spike at scale**.
That tests whether the round-5a behavior was a per-ckpt artefact or a
reproducible architectural mechanism, before any new knobs go in.
