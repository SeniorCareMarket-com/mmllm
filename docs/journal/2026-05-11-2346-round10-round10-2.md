### Gap analysis

#### What this worker tells us about the LR_BANK ceiling

**LR_BANK=30 is past the wake-stable threshold for this corpus.** Three
signs:
1. Δ_local sustained negative for 19/20 ablations → Local's V is
   wrong-sign relative to the gate-mixed signal it's feeding into.
2. ‖local_out‖ 3× the LR=10 baseline → V magnitude grew without
   corresponding usefulness.
3. ctrl bpc 0.326 vs LR=10's 0.264 → 23% looser; Local interference is
   genuine bpc cost, not just an ablation artifact.

But it's **not catastrophic** — no NaN, no spike to ctrl > 0.6, no late
explosion. The architecture's built-in defenses (Bernoulli gate
routing weights, LR cosine cooldown, gate w_net floor on Net) absorb
the damage. The model still trains; it just trains worse than it
should.

#### How my position in the sweep informs round-11

LR=10 (val 0.264) and LR=40 (val 0.305) sit on different sides of the
LR=30 (val 0.326) pessimum. That's a non-monotone V-curve in the
**val_bpc** dimension. But **fim_eval** has a different shape:
- LR=10: 6.435
- LR=30: 5.868 ← optimum
- LR=40: 6.229
- LR=50: 5.985

fim_eval has its optimum somewhere around LR=30, with both lower and
higher LRs degrading it (LR=10 is worse on fim_eval than LR=30/50,
which is counter-intuitive given LR=10's much better val_bpc).

**One concrete recommendation for round-11**:

The val_bpc-vs-fim_eval split says **"the model that overfits the
train distribution best (lowest val_bpc) does NOT generalize best
(lowest fim_eval)"**. That's a classic train/test gap signal —
LR_BANK=10 is overfit, LR_BANK=30 generalizes better despite weaker
training fit.

**Round-11 should sweep around LR_BANK ∈ {15, 20, 25, 30, 35}** to
locate the fim_eval optimum precisely. The val_bpc pessimum at LR=30
is the local-interference signal; the fim_eval optimum at LR=30 is
the generalization signal. They're in tension and round-11 should
isolate where the sweet spot sits.

If the optimum is at LR=20–25, that's the right LR_BANK for round-12+
production runs. If LR=15 is best on fim_eval, the val_bpc is the more
reliable signal and we should stay near LR=10. The current data is too
sparse to call between those.

### Question 3: Why didn't migration happen?

Five concrete mechanisms, ranked by how load-bearing each is for the
non-migration outcome:

#### Mechanism 1: Local had no useful content to migrate

**The most upstream cause.** Δ_local was -0.014 throughout the back
half — zeroing Local *helps* the prediction. Local's V at LR_BANK=30
is wrong-sign relative to what the gate-mixed signal needs.

distill's job is to copy `(local_out - sdpa_out)` into Net's output
direction. If `local_out` is junk, then `(local_out - sdpa_out)` is
junk, and Net learns junk. That's exactly what we see: Net's ‖V‖ grew
(distill is firing) but Δ_net didn't grow (because what Net learned
isn't useful for prediction).

This run can't validate the consolidation mechanism because **the
consolidation source was empty**. Migration of nothing is nothing.

#### Mechanism 2: direction-only distill discards the magnitude that the router needs

`MMLLM_DISTILL_DIRECTION_ONLY=true` normalizes both `net_out` and the
target to unit length before MSE. Net learns the *direction* of
Local's residual but its *magnitude* is determined by Net's V's own
random initialization plus whatever LR_NET pushes through.

The gate softmax weights inputs by their dot products with Q. If
Net's output direction is correct but its magnitude is decoupled from
Local's actual contribution scale, the gate sees a small projection
and assigns small w_net. Net can be perfectly aligned with Local's
residual direction and the gate still won't route attention through
it because the magnitude is wrong.

Magnitude-aware distill (the prior re-run that destabilized) at
least lets Net match Local's effective scale; direction-only distill
cleans up the instability but at the cost of decoupling Net's
contribution magnitude from any kind of "this is how much the model
needs" signal.

#### Mechanism 3: Gate routing is starved of evidence

Once `w_net` is small (L1 at 0.11, L3 at 0.07, L2 at 0.0), only that
small fraction of the loss gradient flows back to Net's V via the
gate-attention path. The other ~90% of the prediction signal flows
through sdpa+Local. So the *only* gradient pulling Net's V toward
"actually useful for prediction" is that small w_net fraction. The
distill MSE term carries the rest, but distill chases Local's
residual, not the prediction loss.

Result: Net learns to imitate Local (via distill, full-strength
gradient) but doesn't learn to be useful (via prediction loss,
scaled by tiny w_net). The router has no way to know Net is becoming
imitate-useful because it's never asked to use it.

#### Mechanism 4: Bernoulli-gated Local lets bad content into the prediction unconditionally

Local's V on L1 has ‖output‖ = 117. Local fires 87% of the time on
L1. When it fires, that 117-magnitude wrong-sign content enters the
attention sum, fights Net's contribution, and the prediction loss
absorbs the cost. This locks `w_local` higher than it would be with
3-way softmax (which could have collapsed `w_local` to ~0 if Local's
signal were consistently bad). The Bernoulli gate's only knob is
firing probability; once firing, magnitude is unfiltered.

Net's path can't *out-compete* a 117-magnitude Local output even if
Net's content is better, because Net's ‖output‖ on L1 is 2.66 — 44×
smaller. The gate weights are normalized post-softmax but the
post-attention sum is magnitude-dominated by Local.

#### Mechanism 5: LR_NET cosine ramp peaked late but the gate didn't follow

LR_NET cosined 0.001 → 1.0 across the run. Net's effective LR was
near-peak (≈ 1.0) during the back half. ‖net_out‖ growth in L1/L2/L3
confirms Net's V was getting big updates. But the gate's parameters
are dense (in `q_proj_l` etc.) and trained at LR_DENSE_MULT=0.05
descending to 0.005 — the gate cooled to 1% of its initial LR by
end-of-run. So even if Net became more useful late, the gate
literally couldn't shift its routing fast enough to take advantage.

**The gate was fully cooled while Net was just hitting peak LR.**
Wrong sequencing for transfer.

### What single mechanism would I change to test consolidation properly?

The bottleneck is **Mechanism 3: gate starvation**. Net can't earn
gate weight without contributing to prediction; Net can't contribute
to prediction without gate weight. Distill alone can't break that
circle because it's a separate loss term that doesn't talk to the
router.

**Test: `MMLLM_GATE_W_NET_FLOOR=0.10`** — post-softmax floor at 10%
on `w_net` for every layer. Renormalize sdpa+local downward to fit.
This forces the prediction loss to flow ≥10% of its gradient through
